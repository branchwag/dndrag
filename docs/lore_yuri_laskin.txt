# Yuri Laskin

## Identity
- Nature/type: Vampire
- Race/species: Unknown (appears human in presentation; became a vampire by choice after falling in love with a female vampire)
- Full name: Yuri Laskin

## Role & Affiliation
- Long-time friend, mentor, and ally of Milly Varna; introduced as operating from her hidden manor
- Covert double agent: secretly corresponded with Madame Yugova Astronovich, a powerful vampire noble aligned with Fraz-Urb'luu's interests
- more recently

## History & Origin
- Became a vampire because he fell in love with a woman who was one; his beloved was subsequently beheaded by the Paladins of Torm, which fueled his deep hatred of them
- A vial of his tears at a Grinostrov shop (Alethera's tear shop) was labeled as shed "at the beheading of his beloved"
- "Raised" Milly (raised her, as she put it — though in what exact sense, as mentor/guardian, is not fully specified)
- Was involved in an elaborate scheme: he had tricked the Ikovian Queen by kidnapping her daughter (he "couldn't have children so kidnapped the Queen's daughter" — context note: the queen's daughter's heritage/identity is a campaign plot thread)
- Had some prior connection to Nikolai (the Ikovian changeling diplomat); do not confuse the two — Yuri is a separate vampire with no changeling nature

## Abilities & Powers
- Vampire abilities: mist form, feeding, superhuman presence, and silent movement (footsteps make no sound)
- Vampiric combat: fought in paladin conflicts, summoning bats during battle
- Dark ritual magic: crushed bone skulls into pentagram dust to ignite black flames and transport people
- Taught Milly the spell that protects against vampirism — and because he created it, he knew how to bypass it
- Poison delivery: coated his fangs in a slow-acting toxin that bypassed Milly's protective anti-vampirism spell, allowing him to poison her blood slowly over time

## Key Relationships
- Milly Varna: mentored her; she developed romantic feelings for him; he betrayed her by conspiring with Madame Yugova
- Madame Yugova Astronovich: his co-conspirator; corresponded with her in secret letters found by the party
- The more recently party: appeared to be an ally working with Milly; revealed as a traitor
- Vladimir Baranovsky (the King): Yuri had previously transformed Vladimir into a werewolf; the letter states "my original intention all those years ago was to capture the wolfish brute after transforming him, but his new form made that unexpectedly difficult"
- The Ikovian Queen's daughter: kept under his watch as a hostage/leverage; "she remains blissfully unaware of these happenings and of her own heritage"

## Key Events (more recently)
- Introduced at Milly's manor as a "tall thin man with silver hair" who lurked in the shadows; Milly called him "a long time friend and mentor"
- Fed on Milly's blood to regain strength after battles; the party witnessed this through a cracked door
- His letter to Madame Yugova was discovered by the party in Yugova's manor during the infiltration; the letter revealed his plan to handle the "heir" (Queen's daughter) and expressed confidence that he could "do what is necessary" if she was no longer useful
- Stole the real piece of the Staff of Retribution from Milly (she gave him a fake, but he realized she knew something when he found the decoy)
- Escaped alongside Madame Yugova when their scheme was exposed; both retreated, still possessing a staff piece

## Important Notes
- Appearance: tall, thin man with silver hair; pointed nails; eyes have "the glint of a hunter"; completely silent footfall; gives the impression of a predator at rest
- Described as a vampire in DM stat block notes
- His betrayal of Milly was a major emotional arc of more recently
- His hatred of the Paladins of Torm is genuine, but his method of fighting them — empowering Madame Yugova and installing Milly as a puppet ruler — was not sanctioned by the Ikovian resistance
- Do not confuse with Nikolai: Nikolai is the Ikovian diplomat who re-turned Alora; Yuri is the vampire who mentored Milly
- Yuri is NOT a changeling — he is a vampire; the changeling is Nikolai, a completely separate character
- His full surname "Laskin" appears in his signed letter: "Truly yours, Yuri Laskin"
