# Crevalon

## Type
- Continent / Kingdom
- Primary setting of previously and primary homeland of more recently antagonists (the Paladins of Siadiff originate here)

## Description
- The Kingdom of Crevalon is a relatively peaceful, prosperous medieval-fantasy realm ruled for many years by the elder King Titus.
- The majority of the kingdom, including the King's castle, rests within the Grudoulan Valley.
- Geography: to the west lies the port city of Aberdeen and the Idrelia Sea; to the east rise the Brotor Mountains bordering the Slaerian Desert; to the north is the Elantir Forest; to the south are the Touven (also spelled Toven) Swamps.
- The kingdom's flower is the poppy.
- No firearms are permitted in Crevalon — the King does not allow guns.
- Vampires are legally tolerated in Crevalon under strict conditions (must not prey on humans), though socially controversial, particularly in Siadiff.
- Necromancy outside of sanctioned academic contexts is forbidden in Crevalon; it is openly practiced in Ikovia.

## Major Cities
- Aberdeen — the primary port city; located to the west on the Idrelia Sea; Crevalon's main point of entry and departure by sea
- Siadiff — holy city; headquarters of the Order of the Golden Lion (paladin order of Torm); the most devoutly religious city in Crevalon
- Diondria — home of Taelreth, the Institute of the Arcane; Crevalon's center of magical scholarship
- Handletare — home of the Sylvanian Academy of Magical Arts (elven military-style magic academy)
- Zaudfast — coastal city; home of the Crevalon Navy base
- Finreld — northern elven city within or adjacent to Crevalon
- Felanor — northern elven city within or adjacent to Crevalon
- Sharnwick — a town in Crevalon; contains a fortune teller named Ange

## Institutions & Landmarks
- Taelreth (Institute of the Arcane) — wizard university/magical institute located in Diondria (NOT Handletare)
- Sylvanian Academy of Magical Arts — elven military-style magic academy located in Handletare (NOT Diondria)
- The Order of the Golden Lion — paladin order dedicated to Torm, headquartered in Siadiff
- Crevalon Navy — naval force with a base of operations in Zaudfast
- The Grudoulan Valley — central valley, location of the King's castle
- Kingdom crest: the crest of Crevalon

## Notable Inhabitants / Factions
- King Titus — long-reigning elder monarch; deeply respected; does not wish to involve Crevalon in war with Celestials or Dragon factions; leaves no dynastic heir, hoping a ruling council would succeed him
- Lord Florian Reiter — vampiric lord with a royal agreement to live in the Elantir Forest; former Paladin of Torm from Siadiff; serves as the King's informal liaison and agent
- Sir Thomas Wright — paladin of Siadiff; becomes the de facto ruler of Crevalon after King Titus's death; enacts an oppressive holy crusade in the name of Torm; is revealed to be possessed by a powerful infernal being
- Alora Venette — court mage of Crevalon; ancient vampire; centuries-old arcane figure
- The Aviary — a shadowy criminal/assassin organization operating within Crevalon; their leader, the Dove, is revealed to be the Queen of Air and Darkness (a Fey entity) posing as Titania
- The Solarian Order — sun-worshipping monks who arrived via ship in Aberdeen; antagonists in the campaign; seek to awaken imprisoned dragons and expand their influence
- The Lunarians — mysterious moon-elf forest tribe living in the Elantir Forest; worship Selune; led by Lady Kuyomi (revealed to be a Solar/celestial being); keep to themselves but will respond to provocation

## History & Lore
- The DragonMage War (thousands of years before the campaign): The world was wracked by war between dragon factions — those serving the Lunarian Queen (black, blue, green, silver, white dragons) and those serving the Solarian King (brass, bronze, copper, gold, red dragons). The war created the FrostLands (white dragon's icy breath in the north) and the Slaerian Desert (red dragon's fire). A union of the most powerful mages sealed the dragons away. The Lunarian and Solarian leaders declared a truce, and day/night were divided equally.
- Ancient female mage sealed the dragons individually; each dragon was imprisoned within a magical orb or gem artifact. These artifacts have been passed down or hidden across the world. The Solarians seek to recover them and awaken the dragons to resume the war.
- During previously: King Titus passes away without an heir. Sir Thomas Wright (possessed by an infernal being serving Bel of Avernus) seizes control of Crevalon through the Order of the Golden Lion and launches a crusade that expands east across the Slaerian Desert into Ikovia.

## Connections
- Borders the Slaerian Desert (east) and the Idrelia Sea (west)
- Connected to Ikovia via overland routes through the Slaerian Desert and by sea
- The Elantir Forest connects to the Lunarian Court (a magical moonlit city accessible through a lake within the forest)
- Felanor and Finreld are northern elven cities within or adjacent to Crevalon
- Anearios is a separate continent across the Idrelia Sea to the west

## Important Notes
- Crevalon is the primary setting for previously; more recently takes place largely in Ikovia but the paladins of Siadiff (Crevalon) are the antagonists
- King Titus's death and Thomas Wright's rise mark the transition point between past and present events
- The Solarian monks who provoke conflicts in the campaign are later revealed to be manipulated by a third power (Bel/Avernus-connected infernal beings)
- The Elantir Forest is where Florian Reiter's manor is located — it is NOT a dungeon or hostile zone by default; it is guarded by centaurs and inhabited by the Lunarians
