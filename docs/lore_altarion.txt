# Altarion (Sir Altarion)

## Identity
- Paladin (Order of Torm / Order of the Golden Lion)
- Race: Elf (described as "an elven man, clad in the armor of The Order of the Golden Lion" when he first arrives at Florian's manor)
- Title: Sir Altarion
- Player character (joined the party mid-campaign, played by Joe)

## Role & Affiliation
- Member of the Order of the Golden Lion (the Order of Torm, based in the Holy City of Siadiff)
- Described as Sir Godwin's "right hand" within the Order
- Dispatched by Sir Godwin to assist the party ("I dispatch you to join those within the King's service")
- Entered the party late into the campaign as an additional paladin operative

## History & Origin
- Has performed exorcisms in Siadiff — the DM notes describe him as recognizing the look of possession from "the exorcisms you performed back in Siadiff"
- His family (mother and sister) was kidnapped by the Kyokhans: a people native to the Greylands who believe they can purify the scorched land via human sacrifice
- His mother and sister were burned alive on wooden stakes by the Kyokhans while Altarion was forced to watch, hidden from sight and unable to intervene
- Crevalon forces arrived and saved Altarion but were too late to save his family
- This backstory was revisited in a vision/nightmare sequence during the final campaign arc (pit fiend illusion)

## Abilities & Powers
- Paladin abilities: radiant energy, healing, Lay on Hands equivalent; used to heal party wounds in the Tomb of Jaiyana Saihan while the others fought Virion
- Exorcism rituals: trained in performing exorcisms at Siadiff; was the one to perform the exorcism on Madame Alora when she was possessed by a pit fiend (via the cursed shard)
- Was used his title and authority with the Order to gain access (mentioned: "Altarion flexed his title with the Order along with his biceps")
- Used brute strength to seal the previous room shut (Wall of Stone combined with physical action)
- Received an enchanted Handletarian longsword from Florian (Florian procured it via Lady Orvir as a gift)

## Key Relationships
- Sir Godwin: his superior and the person who dispatched him; Sir Godwin considers him his right hand; Altarion has been in Godwin's study "many times before"
- Florian Reiter: complex relationship — Altarion arrived wary of vampires but Florian gifted him a sword and provided tactical training; Florian's butler Andrei told Altarion the story of how Florian was turned; Altarion reflected on this and moderated his view
- Madame Alora (Alora Venyette): relationship that ended in marriage at the campaign's end, though it ultimately did not last; the initial tension came from Altarion's prejudice against vampires, which Alora proved wrong over time; Alora's feelings toward him were complicated — he reminded her of her earlier husband, toward whom she has a soft spot, but she did not agree with how paladins treat people like her; when possessed by the pit fiend, she attacked him (96 Blight damage); after his exorcism, her face was severely burned by his radiant energy; Alora commented "You don't think I'm a bit old for you?" — a sign of warmth beneath the friction; Altarion's prejudice softened as she proved herself to him
- The party: joined mid-campaign; participated in the Tomb of Jaiyana Saihan, the Greylands arc, the Adelanto hippogriff races, the Avernus arc, and the Underdark endgame
- Malek (Scirocco's brother): tied with Scirocco in the hippogriff races in Adelanto, leaving Malek in last place

## Key Events (campaign)
- Arrived at Florian's manor dispatched by Sir Godwin to assist with defeating Virion; initial discomfort working with vampires; convinced by the party to set aside this discomfort
- Fought alongside the party in the Tomb of Jaiyana Saihan: healed party wounds while Scirocco and Elly took down Virion's form; Altarion then recovered the shard of celestial essence
- Was attacked by Madame Alora in the night when she was possessed by the pit fiend from the cursed shard; suffered 96 points of Blight damage; made a CON saving throw; performed the exorcism that drove out the pit fiend (severely burning Alora's face in the process)
- Scene where Alora apologized to Altarion after the exorcism (DM note: "Scene where Alora apologizes to Altarion")
- Accompanied the party to Adelanto; participated in the hippogriff races with Scirocco (they tied; Malek came last)
- Traveled to the Greylands with the party; encountered bird-like creatures that turned him to stone; was restored and then traced celestial essence using his paladin senses
- Traveled to Avernus with the party; was enraptured by a pit fiend illusion showing his family burning on pyres
- Traveled through the Underdark in the final arc (Caeda, Ari, and Altarion); repeatedly triggered traps (poison darts, fear gas, brown mold); helped place The Sword of Avernus, Scirocco's scimitar, and his own weapon to open the final chamber
- Was a bit late to one meeting having taken a moment to speak with Alora
- Married Alora Venyette at the campaign's end; the marriage did not ultimately last

## Important Notes
- Altarion joined the party LATE — he is not present from the beginning; he arrives partway through, dispatched by Sir Godwin
- Sir Godwin is his superior, NOT his equal; Altarion's rank is below Godwin's
- Alora remembers attacking Altarion while she was possessed — it is a specific memory she carries
- His family's deaths (burned by the Kyokhans in the Greylands) are a core part of his backstory and were used against him in a vision sequence in Avernus
- He began with a prejudice against vampires (Florian and Alora); both proved him wrong; Florian gifted him a sword and Alora proved herself throughout the campaign
- He and Alora married at the campaign's end; the arc began with his prejudice and her complicated feelings (he reminded her of her earlier husband, but she disagreed with paladin treatment of people like her); the marriage did not last
- He is an elf — this makes his prejudice against vampires notable since Florian is also of a similar physical type (elven-looking, pale, silvery hair)
- Altarion wears PALADIN ARMOR — specifically the armor of the Order of the Golden Lion; he does NOT wear a tuxedo or any formal civilian attire; he is a paladin and is always described as armored
