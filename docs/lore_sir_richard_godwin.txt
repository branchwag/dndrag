# Sir Richard Godwin

## Identity
- Paladin of Torm
- Race: Human; described as having slicked-back black hair, heavily armored
- Title: Sir Godwin; full name Sir Richard Godwin
- Voice described as "low British" in DM notes

## Role & Affiliation
- Paladin of Siadiff; member of the Order of the Golden Lion (Knights of Torm)
- Attended the formal King's council at Taelreth representing Siadiff
- Siadiff is a holy city built on a hill overlooking the sea; all buildings are white stone; Siadiff paladins are described as pious, expert demon-hunters, and sometimes "overzealous"
- His predecessor in Siadiff was Florian Reiter (before Florian was turned)

## History & Origin
- He knows Florian Reiter as his predecessor; when the party mentions Florian's story, Sir Godwin says: "Ah yes a sad tale indeed. They were sent on a mission to hunt down a spellcaster that was attempting to summon a being from the Nine Hells. Unfortunately they all perished and to this day no one appears to know the cause."
- He was unaware Florian survived as a vampire until Campaign 1; upon discovering Florian was alive and in the King's presence he was "livid"
- He sees vampires and all creatures of the night as irredeemable evil; sees the world in black and white on this front

## Abilities & Powers
- Armored paladin with significant holy combat ability
- Can deploy radiant whips ("radiant whips wound themselves around Florian's limbs and Sir Godwin stepped out of the shadows" at the masquerade)
- Expert in demon-hunters' arts; the party was directed to Siadiff for expertise on devils and demons
- Performs or oversees exorcisms

## Key Relationships
- Florian Reiter: Sir Godwin is Florian's successor in the Order; openly hostile to Florian; they glare each other down across the King's council table; Sir Godwin uses the term "the devil" when referring to Florian
- King Titus: formal servant and paladin to the King; frequently disagrees with the King's decisions (especially regarding Florian's presence and the use of Crevalon forces against the dragon threats)
- The party (Campaign 1): worked alongside them at the King's emergency council; does not trust them but cooperates under the King's authority
- Sir Thomas Wright: both are paladins of Siadiff; Sir Thomas Wright is another paladin in the Order (later becomes the big bad in Campaign 2)

## Key Events (campaign)
- Campaign 1: Met the party at Siadiff when they came seeking information on the puzzle box and Silvester; provided information that angels can fall into the Hells, that Silvester likely has another true name, and that devils look to corrupt souls for the infernal army
- Attended the King's emergency council at Taelreth after the red dragon attack; argued against enlisting more soldiers (feared panic) and against dispatching Crevalon forces; was overruled by the King on multiple counts
- Deployed radiant whips to apprehend Florian at Lady Vesemir's masquerade ball
- Stormed out of the council angrily when the King allowed Florian's suggestions to stand

## Important Notes
- Sir Godwin sees the world in black and white: "Sir Godwin tends to see the world in black and white. Anything with so much of a trace of [evil]…"
- He is described as a "sanctimonious asshole" by one of the party members
- Not to be confused with Sir Thomas Wright (another Siadiff paladin who becomes Campaign 2's villain); they are distinct characters
- Godwin was particularly hostile about the King "allowing a vampire in his presence" (referring to Florian)
- He views Taelreth's school of necromancy as something Siadiff disapproves of
