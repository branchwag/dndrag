# Lady Adrastea Orvir

## Identity
- Mage (wizard specializing in planar magic; knows spells up to at least 7th level including Plane Shift)
- Race: Elf (high elf; described as having "delicate" features and part of a noble ancient elven lineage)
- Also known as: "Lady Orvir"; full name "Lady Adrastea Orvir"
- Alias used in the text: "Adrastea" (used interchangeably with "Lady Orvir")
- She is a noble, from an ancient and prestigious elven family

## Role & Affiliation
- Benefactor and Overseer of the Sylvanian Academy of Magical Arts in Handletare
- Most of the Academy's library volumes were donated from her family's collection
- Acts as a de-facto librarian/archivist in addition to her overseer role
- Continues working at the Academy even as overseer ("Adrastea continues to be a librarian at the Academy")
- Her father Erius Orvir is mentioned as heading a magic shop in Handletare (in his spare time, obsessed with finding her mother)
- Is a descendant of one of the mages that locked away the dragons 4,000 years ago

## History & Origin
- Her mother Gwentharidel Orvir (Gwen) was a talented elven mage who ventured to another plane to study it and never returned; Lady Orvir has gathered her mother's notes and believes she is on the Plane of Air (in the City of Avis) or possibly the Plane of Fire (City of Brass)
- Gwen was feebleminded by Virion Maganti on another plane; she had gotten caught up in the conflict while trying to find Khadja (the imprisoned djinn)
- The grandfather (Khadja's family) ordered Gwen to be imprisoned after losing Khadja; Gwen is forbidden to be healed and remains in imprisonment there
- Her father became a recluse after losing her mother, wanting nothing to do with anyone; he obsesses over finding Gwen
- Lady Orvir is hesitant to go to another plane alone to retrieve her mother because if she dies it would destroy her father
- It has been one to several years since her mother disappeared

## Abilities & Powers
- Knows Plane Shift (7th level spell) or is on the verge of that breakthrough
- Has access to extensive magical tomes and artifacts through her family's collection
- Capable of stopping time (noted in the masquerade ball scene: "Adrastea stops time")
- Has a pocket dimension she stores books in (waved her hand and a book dissipated into a pocket dimension)
- Described as having "phenomenal sense of intuition"
- One notable magic item in her collection: Manual of Quickness of Action

## Key Relationships
- Ari Crownsilver: she and Ari have a developing romantic connection throughout Campaign 1; they met at the Sylvanian Academy, went on a "date" to the confectionary, danced together at Lady Vesemir's masquerade ball; Ari is deeply smitten with her ("Lady Orvir haunted his thoughts"); she shows genuine warmth and care toward him
- Florian Reiter: met Lady Orvir at one of Lady Vesemir's annual parties; views her in a non-romantic way but with great respect; worried about her safety and asked Ari to check in on her
- Lady Vesemir: they move in the same social circles; attend the same annual masquerade parties
- Rathilion Lorin: the Head Arcanist at the Sylvanian Academy; works beneath her in the Academy hierarchy; they were both present at Lady Vesemir's masquerade
- Erius Orvir: her father; described as obsessed and somewhat kooky after losing her mother; he is mentioned in connection with a magic shop and possibly the "wizard in the north" (Frostlands tower, though this is an alternate suggestion in the notes)
- Virion Maganti: indirectly connected — he feebleminded her mother; this is a source of grief and urgency for Lady Orvir

## Key Events (campaign)
- Campaign 1: Party first encounters her at the Sylvanian Academy in Handletare; she shows them the library (an immense room with impossible vertical shelves) and translates an archaic text naming Silvester's true nature as a servant of Bel
- Read aloud the text identifying Silvester as a pit fiend servant of Bel, the Warlord of Avernus
- Attended Lady Vesemir's masquerade ball as Ari's companion (wore an owl mask); stopped time during a critical moment at the ball
- After Virion's attack on one occasion, it is noted that "Virion goes after Lady Orvir at one point. Next time Ari goes to visit her." — suggesting she was targeted or threatened
- Her note to Ari (a beacon of blue light / arcane message) serves as a plot device

## Important Notes
- Lady Adrastea Orvir is NOT connected to the dwarves of Doragon; those dwarves are connected to Alora Venyette
- Lady Orvir and Madame Alora Venyette are entirely separate characters; they share no identity
- The Sylvanian Academy of Magical Arts in Handletare is her institution, NOT Taelreth (Taelreth is in Diondria and is a rival institution)
- She is described as a noble: "Lady Adrastea Orvir (she is actually a noble)"
- Florian explicitly asked Ari to "check in on Lady Orvir" for him, saying "I don't want anything to happen to her" — indicating Florian has protective feelings toward her
- A note in the text mentions "Minerva Orvir" as a possible female mage who died during the dragon mage war serving the Lunarian Queen and may be related to Adrastea's family; this is an additional family connection
