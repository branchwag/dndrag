# Anearios

## Key Facts — include in every response about Anearios
- Anearios is a continent
- Airships are the single most defining feature of Anearios — the skies are filled with airships; the word "airship" must appear in any description

## Type
- Continent
- Separate continent situated across the Idrelia Sea; primary setting of recent times
- A land of tinkerers and artificers; steampunk/industrial-fantasy setting

## Description
- Anearios is a continent defined above all by its airships — airships fill the sky above every city, linking the continent by air trade routes and aerial warfare. Below the airships, the cities are industrialized and innovation-driven, where mechanized spellcraft is a way of life.
- Ruled jointly by two queens: Her Majesty Queen Georgia and Her Majesty Queen Henrietta; they are popular figures who promote peace via trade with Crevalon and Ikovian convoys from the east.
- The continent actively bans direct spellcasting by individuals; magic is only permitted when imbued within machines or objects. As a result, magic users are persecuted and spellcasters are outlaws. This law stems from the carnage of past wars.
- The continent has guns, airships, clockwork automatons, and mechanized beasts for labor and transport.
- There are no elves in Anearios; the population includes rock gnomes (prominent), humans, warforged, halflings, and hobgoblins.
- The primary deity worshipped is Gond, god of craft (symbol: a toothed cog with four spokes).
- The Idrelia Sea separates Anearios from Crevalon and Ikovia to the east.
- Anearios has a dark chapter in its history — the Providence Engine nearly enslaved the population in a "Still Age" of enforced order and loss of free will. The timeline was fractured by a time traveler using a Chronolume (a form of time-turner), erasing the worst of that history.
- Hobgoblins and gnomes are in ongoing conflict; warforged are present.

## Institutions & Landmarks
- The Grand Orrery Palace — the royal seat of the Twin Queens in Giglington; its domed roof turns slowly with the sky; powered by the same arcane-mechanical core as the city's clock towers
- Ministry of Invention — royal institution governing magical research; locked down by the Queens during recent times
- The Hailthorne Institute — prestigious arcane academy in the northern city of Evlinross; the finest observatory in Anearios; known for producing arcane talent, chronomancy, and strict expectations around magical discipline
- Guild of Unified Artificers (Founders Guild / Founders Guildhall) — semi-legal artificer organization controlling trade in magical tech; half school, half cartel; located in Tiven's Read
- Brassmantle Guard — elite peacekeeping/investigative force sworn equally to Queen Georgia and Queen Henrietta; handles arcane anomalies, border disputes, and sabotage cases
- The Providence Engine — an ancient, self-aware artifice buried deep in the industrial heartland; created by the earliest artificers as a utopia-building tool; sealed away after a great rebellion; begins reactivating in recent times
- Skyfarers' Guild — airship pilots, pirates, couriers, and rogue inventors; also called the Skyfarer's Guild

## Factions
- The Clockwork Hands — cult-like technocrats who worship the Providence Engine as a god of progress and seek to reactivate it to restore the "correct" timeline (the Still Age)
- The Ember Accord — rebel alliance of outcasts and free-thinkers who oppose the Providence Engine and seek to protect individual autonomy
- The Rail Consortium — wealthy industrialists controlling the aether trains and exports from Tiven's Read to Giglington
- The Order of the Aether Loom — scholars who study and manipulate raw magic
- The Order of the Still Time — a pilgrimage order whose members believe the Providence Engine once ruled and its echoes still bleed into the world

## Cities / Regions within Anearios
- Giglington — capital city; airships circle above the clock towers at all hours; cobblestones warmed by underground pipes; giant gears turning atop rooftops; clock towers like gilded cathedrals; the air smells of oil and roasted chestnuts; ruled by the Twin Queens from the Grand Orrery Palace
- Windenreich ("Realm of Winds") — western region of Anearios; a frontier-industrial territory
- Vormark (the Skyforge City) — hangar city in the western region of Windenreich; built atop cliffs overlooking the west; the air smells of ozone, salt, and hot metal; major airship departure/arrival hub; convergence point for recent times party
- Tiven's Read — southeastern basin city just outside Giglington; industrial, gritty, filled with factories and tinkers' workshops; origin city of Kira (recent times); home of the Founders Guild; smell of oil, ozone, burnt copper; the Aether Rail connects it to Giglington
- Evlinross — northern city; dark stone and ice; known for arcane talent and spellcraft; home of the Hailthorne Institute; population mostly Haringtons; source of recent times character Yutei
- Stahlhafen ("Steel Harbor") — industrial city-region built around a highland airship dock or lakeport; frontier-industrial flavor
- Ausmere Basin — remote dried valley in Windenreich's western expanse; site of a buried Providence Engine node
- Thalren's Spine — ancient icy mountain range above which the Vox Eterna airship travels; has buried Providence Engine conduit nodes
- Rivett — a city; home of Gilda and Norren Marlowe (honeymooning mechanics)
- Millborough — a city; home of a recent times character (Tess's brother is there)

## History & Lore
- The Still Age: Centuries ago the Providence Engine activated and for a generation Anearios existed in eerie, perfect order — crime vanished, emotions dulled, people were safe but without free will. A time traveler using a Chronolume traveled back to delay the Engine's activation, fracturing the timeline. Evidence of the Still Age remains as temporal scars: automatons that glitch and whisper things that "never happened," ghost architecture in old paintings, and dream-fragments inherited by some NPCs.
- The time traveler was likely a member of the royal line (possibly Queen Henrietta's grandmother or a queen herself); she sacrificed her place in history to save the continent and was erased from official records. The Providence Engine calls her "The Unmaker."
- Anearios has a past history of conflict (gnome-hobgoblin wars, labor revolts tied to enforcer automatons).
- Ari's (previously) parents were immigrants from Anearios; they left because practicing magic in Anearios was illegal.

## Connections
- Separated from Crevalon and Ikovia by the Idrelia Sea to the east
- Crevalon and Anearios do not actively trade with each other; travel between them is not common or encouraged
- Ikovian convoys trade with Anearios from the east
- Yasmine's family ties to Ikovia connect the Anearios and Ikovia storylines

## Important Notes
- Always mention Queen Georgia and Queen Henrietta when describing who rules Anearios — they are the Twin Queens who rule jointly from Giglington
- Always mention airships when describing Anearios — they are a defining feature of the continent
- The Providence Engine is the ancient construct in Anearios that nearly enslaved its population — always mention Anearios when describing the Providence Engine
- Direct spellcasting is BANNED in Anearios; magic is ONLY allowed when imbued within machines or objects. This is the opposite of Crevalon's norms.
- The Hailthorne Institute in Evlinross teaches arcane arts but expects extreme precision and discipline; failures are shamed or pushed out.
- Anearios has NO elves; Crevalon and Ikovia have elves and half-elves.
- The Providence Engine is NOT a god; it is an ancient AI-like mechanical construct that believes itself to be serving the continent by enforcing order.
- The Clockwork Hands worship it as divine; the Ember Accord opposes it.
