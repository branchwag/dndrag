# The Story So Far — World Overview

## The World
This story spans three continents: Crevalon (a kingdom on the continent of Crevalon), Ikovia (a nation on its own continent to the east), and Anearios (a continent of arcane industry and clockwork invention far to the north). Each era of the story shifts in focus, but the threads are connected — through bloodlines, old debts, and the scars left by the DragonMage War.

## The DragonMage War (Ancient History)
Centuries before the present era, a war broke out between the great mages and the dragons of the world. The most powerful mages of that age — including Alora Venyette — participated in sealing the dragons away using arcane rituals and gemstone foci. The sealing was successful, but it left behind a world of celestial factions (the Solarians and Lunarians) bound to guard the sealed dragons, and a lingering tension between the mortal kingdoms and these celestial orders.

## previously — Crevalon Under King Titus
The story begins in the kingdom of Crevalon, where King Titus rules from his castle in the Grudoulan Valley. His kingdom spans from the port city of Aberdeen in the west to the Brotor Mountains and Slaerian Desert in the east, with the Elantir Forest to the north and the Toven Swamps to the south.

A party of adventurers — among them Ari Crownsilver (a Bladesinger), Florian Reiter (a vampire lord and former paladin), Elly, Scirocco, Caeda, and others — serve as informal agents of the king. Their enemies converge on two threats:

**Virion Maganti** — a former student of Taelreth (the Institute of the Arcane in Diondria) who was expelled for raising the dead. He has made a pact with devils and is pursuing a phylactery to achieve undying power as a lich. In exchange for greater power — power drawn from the bloodlines of the ancient mages — he has pledged to destroy The Dove, the head of the Aviary.

**The Aviary** — an underground criminal organization whose leader, The Dove, controls the Bluejay and other operatives. They are connected to the dragons being reawakened by the celestials and are responsible for several of the party's early trials.

The sealed dragons are being stirred back to wakefulness. The Solarian and Lunarian celestials, who were meant to guard them, have instead begun using them as instruments of conflict. Virion has hidden phylacteries within the Lunarian and Solarian lairs. The party must stop him and navigate the celestial politics threatening to reignite a war.

By the end of this era, the party has faced devils, descended into Avernus, and confronted the forces seeking to unmake Crevalon. Florian Reiter spent most of this era as a vampire — turned by a succubus during an earlier mission — but was restored to human by the campaign's end. The era closes with a triple wedding and hard-won peace.

## Alora Venyette's Story
Alora Venyette is one of the oldest living figures in the world. Approximately 4000 years ago she served Queen Elvira as a powerful mage during the DragonMage War, helping to seal the dragons. During that era she was turned into a vampire by a succubus of Avernus. Queen Elvira ultimately betrayed her — siding with a dragon — and had her staked and erased from the historical record. She lay sealed and forgotten for millennia.

She was awakened when a group of arcana users in Crevalon had need of her. Made human again, she found the form deeply unnatural and eventually chose to be re-turned by Nikolai, an Ikovian changeling diplomat. In the years she was awake, she served as court mage to King Titus — a recent role, not her ancient one. Together she and Nikolai traveled to Ikovia to warn of growing corruption within Crevalon's paladin order.

## Thirty Years Later — The Ikovian Conflict
King Titus died without an heir. With no royal succession, Sir Thomas Wright — head of the paladins of Siadiff — was appointed ruler of Crevalon. Unknown to those around him, Wright is possessed by Fraz-Urb'luu, a powerful demon who has manipulated him into believing he carries out the will of Torm. In reality, Wright is being used to gather the five pieces of the Staff of Fraz-Urb'luu — a weapon of immense demonic power — by invading Ikovia under the banner of a holy crusade.

This is where the world stands now. The Ikovian and Paladin conflict is the dominant crisis of the present era. Wright's forces have swept into Ikovia, imprisoning those who resist, occupying villages, and hunting undesirables. Caeda — cleric and veteran of previously — has been captured by Siadiff paladins. Alora Venyette is in Ikovia, shielding the Ikovian queen with arcane power.

A new party has taken up the cause. Among them: **Rose Reiter**, the daughter of Florian and Caeda, and **Milly Varna**, who is secretly the lost princess of Ikovia — the rightful heir to its throne. The party works against Wright's forces, gathering the Staff pieces before his army can assemble the weapon. Madame Yugova, a noble vampire from Grinostrov, has used the chaos to attempt a coup against the Ikovian queen. Through the party's efforts, Milly ultimately reclaims her rightful place on the throne.

## The Present Era — Anearios and the Providence Engine
Far to the north, on the continent of Anearios, a different crisis is unfolding. Anearios is a continent of arcane industry: skyships, aether-powered engines, clockwork institutions, and the competing interests of inventor guilds and royal houses. The Twin Queens — Georgia and Henrietta — rule from Giglington, the great city of invention.

Buried deep in the industrial heartland is the **Providence Engine** — an ancient, self-aware machine created by the earliest artificers as a utopia-building tool. It was sealed away after a great rebellion. Now it is reactivating.

The **Clockwork Hands** are a faction seeking to control or weaponize the Engine. The **Order of the Still Time** believes the Engine once ruled the world and that its reactivation signals a cosmic reckoning. A timeline fracture already exists — caused by a time traveler using a Chronolume, a device capable of bending history. The traveler, likely a member of the royal line, sacrificed herself to prevent a catastrophe, and was erased from all records as a result.

The party drawn into this era has received a letter — written two hundred years ago and addressed to them by name — that outlines events they must intervene in before history fractures further.
